Comparing Thanksgiving and Eid

Thanksgiving and Eid are both important holidays, but they are quite different.

Thanksgiving is celebrated in the United States in November. Families eat turkey, mashed potatoes, and pumpkin pie. The holiday is shorter than Eid - it's only one day (plus the weekend). People usually stay home and watch football on TV.

Eid is celebrated by Muslims around the world. Eid al-Fitr is longer than Thanksgiving - it lasts three days! The food is sweeter because people eat many desserts and cookies. People visit relatives and give money to children.

Both holidays are more traditional than regular days. Family is important for both celebrations, but Eid is more religious than Thanksgiving.
